# Assessment Form Creative Territories

## Direction 1: Calibration Console

- Concept: a focused command-center tool for moving through assessment questions.
- Layout: desktop rail plus question panel; mobile progress strip plus sticky action.
- Material: white panels, ink type, subtle line texture, violet/cyan state accents.
- Motion: progress sweep, option confirmation, question panel reveal.
- Fit: strongest because it solves the space problem directly.

## Direction 2: Conversational Index

- Concept: each answer feels like a compact line in a guided interview.
- Layout: text-led, low-chrome vertical sequence.
- Material: nearly flat surfaces and natural-language prompts.
- Motion: minimal answer reveal and step change.
- Fit: very usable, but less distinctive for LitmusAI.

## Direction 3: Signal Lab

- Concept: the learner is tuning five AI-readiness signals.
- Layout: domain signal badges with a larger animated meter.
- Material: darker lab surface with luminous accent data.
- Motion: domain meter pulses and scoreline transitions.
- Fit: memorable, but risks feeling too theatrical for a practical assessment.

## Recommended Direction

- Selected direction: Calibration Console
- Weighted rubric score: Design 8.4, Usability 9.0, Creativity 8.1, Content 8.3; weighted total 8.55.
- Why it wins: it visibly changes the form architecture while keeping the assessment fast and legible.
- Improvement before implementation: keep motion small and avoid making the left rail decorative.
